The Team

Georgia Tech Off-Road is a student-led team founded in 1998 that participates in annual Baja SAE competitions. The team currently has 35 undergraduate members with students majoring mostly in some form of engineering. Students on the team are provided with an opportunity to work on a large scale and time-constrained engineering project. They learn to not only design and analyze vehicle components, but also to develop communication and teamwork skills in ensuring proper integration between designs.

Competition

The North America Baja SAE competitions take place at three locations each year and have upwards of 100 teams at each site. Student teams are tasked with designing and fabricating a high-performing single-seat off-terrain vehicle to sell to the enthusiast market. Teams compete in a set of static and dynamic events that span evaluations from vehicle designs to business models. Professionals from industry are involved with the competition and serve as judges to assess the student teams.

Dynamic Events

The dynamic events are pure tests of the vehicle's performance. Events in this category generally include an acceleration event, suspension event, maneuverability event, and an endurance event. In addition, depending on the competition either a rock crawl, sled pull, or hill climb. Each of these events tests a different part of the vehicle's performance and scores are given out accordingly. The endurance event is by far the largest and most exciting event, comprising of a four hour long race across a track filled with various off-terrain obstacles. The goal of this event is to earn the most amount of laps.

Static Events

A design judging, sales presentation, and a cost event make up the static events. Professional engineers from industry examine and score team designs based on the adherence to the formal engineering cycle. To score high points, teams must show they have not only performed proper analysis on their designs, but have also validated and verified the designs through rigorous testing. Teams also pitch a business model for their prototype vehicle, where final manufacturing costs and prototype costs are considered.

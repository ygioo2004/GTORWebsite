Competition

The Baja SAE competitions provide university students with an opportunity to apply theory learned in the classroom environment to real world applications. Student teams are tasked with designing and fabricating a high-performing single-seat off-terrain vehicle to sell to the enthusiast market. Teams compete in a set of static and dynamic events that span evaluations from vehicle designs and performance to business models. Professionals from industry are involved with the competition and serve as judges.

Dynamic Events

The dynamic events are pure tests of the vehicle's performance. Events in this category generally include an acceleration event, suspension event, maneuverability event, and an endurance event. In addition, depending on the competition either a rock crawl, sled pull, or hill climb.

Static Events

A design judging, sales presentation, and a cost event make up the static events. Professional engineers from industry examine and score team designs based on the adherence to the formal engineering cycle. Teams also pitch a business model for their prototype vehicle, where final manufacturing costs and prototype costs are considered.

The Team

Georgia Tech Off-Road was founded in 1998 by a group of students specifically interested in getting hands on engineering experience. Since then, there have been numerous changes in vehicle builds, but the drive to perform well in competitions has always existed.
